The songs your crowd wants to hear

Three 45-minute sets per night, with a 30-minute dinner break. We rotate songs so the same crowd doesn't hear the same song twice.

How we build a set

Three categories per set:

  1. Openers — high-energy, recognizable, gets people on the floor fast
  2. Middle — sing-alongs, slower songs, ballads for couples
  3. Closer — anthems, dance-till-they-kick-you-out energy

You tell us your crowd (age range, vibe, type of room) and we tailor the mix.

Holiday set (Nov–Dec only)

If your gig is in November or December, ask about our holiday rotation — we add about 15 Christmas classics (White Christmas, Feliz Navidad, Run Run Rudolph, Jingle Bell Rock, etc.) and rotate them in.

Songs we don't play (honest list)

We don't do:

  • Anything newer than about 2010 (we're a classic band, not a current-hits band)
  • Heavy metal (no Metallica, no Pantera)
  • Country from before 1990 (no Hank Williams Sr., no George Jones)
  • Anything that requires a horn section (no Chicago, no Blood Sweat & Tears)

If your crowd wants something specific that's not on the list, ask Buck. We add new songs when we get requests, especially if we have a few weeks' notice before the gig.

Set length & break structure

Dinner break: 30 min between sets 2 and 3. We eat whatever you're feeding the staff.

Short gigs (under 2 hours): We compress to two sets, no break.

Long gigs (4+ hours): Add a fourth short set or extend sets with extra covers. Buck will work it out with you when you book.
